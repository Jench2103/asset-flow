# AssetFlow

**A macOS app for snapshot-based portfolio management and asset allocation tracking.**

AssetFlow helps you take control of your investments by recording portfolio snapshots over time. Instead of syncing with brokerage accounts, you capture the state of your portfolio whenever you choose — giving you a clear, private picture of how your assets are allocated and how they grow. Everything stays on your Mac, with no cloud accounts required.

## Feature Highlights

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

- :material-camera:{ .lg .middle } **Snapshot-Based Tracking**

    ---

    Record your portfolio's state at any point in time. Compare snapshots to see how your holdings change across weeks, months, or years.

- :material-currency-usd:{ .lg .middle } **Multi-Currency Support**

    ---

    Track assets in multiple currencies with automatic exchange rate fetching. All values convert to your base currency for a unified view.

- :material-tag-outline:{ .lg .middle } **Category & Target Allocation**

    ---

    Organize assets into categories like Equities, Bonds, and Cash. Set target allocation percentages to define your ideal portfolio.

- :material-scale-balance:{ .lg .middle } **Rebalancing Calculator**

    ---

    See exactly what to buy or sell to reach your target allocation. Take the guesswork out of portfolio rebalancing.

- :material-file-import-outline:{ .lg .middle } **CSV Import**

    ---

    Bulk import assets and cash flows from spreadsheets. Get up and running quickly with data you already have.

- :material-chart-line:{ .lg .middle } **Performance Metrics**

    ---

    Track growth rate, Modified Dietz return, time-weighted return (TWR), and compound annual growth rate (CAGR).

- :material-chart-pie:{ .lg .middle } **Charts & Visualizations**

    ---

    Interactive pie charts for allocation breakdowns and line charts for portfolio value over time.

- :material-lock:{ .lg .middle } **Security**

    ---

    Optional app lock with Touch ID, Apple Watch, or password. Your financial data stays private.

</div>
